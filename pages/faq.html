Native plants are species that occur naturally in a particular region, ecosystem, or habitat without human introduction. They are adapted to the local climate, soil, and environmental conditions.

Native plants support local wildlife (like birds, bees, and butterflies), require less water and maintenance, and help preserve regional biodiversity.

Check local nurseries, specialized native plant societies, or environmental organizations in your region. Our "Select Plant" page can also provide region-specific recommendations!

Once established, most native plants require very little supplemental watering. However, during the first year after planting, regular watering is crucial to help them develop strong root systems.

They actually create a balance! While some insects eat plants, native plants also attract the predators (like birds and ladybugs) that eat those pests, reducing the need for chemical pesticides.

Yes! Many native species thrive in containers. Just ensure you choose plants appropriate for the pot size and that your containers have adequate drainage.
