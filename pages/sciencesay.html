Biodiversity & Wildlife

Native plant gardens significantly increase local biodiversity. Studies on Certified Wildlife HabitatsÂ® show they support more animal species, including almost twice as many bird species, compared to traditional garden spaces.

This increased variety is due to native plants providing the specialized food and shelter local wildlife relies on, which non-native ornamentals often fail to offer.

Climate Impact (CO2)

Native plant habitats contribute significantly to climate change mitigation. Properties with native landscaping have been found to sequester carbon dioxide (CO2) at a much higher rate than non-certified properties.

The increase in tree coverage and healthy native vegetation aids in improving water retention, minimizing erosion, and actively reducing atmospheric carbon and local air pollution.

Essential Pollinator Support

Small-scale native plant gardens are proven to boost pollinator abundance and diversity. Native plants provide the critical timing and nutrient quality of nectar, pollen, and host plants that local butterflies, bees, and other insects need to thrive.

This support is most effective when the garden incorporates a variety of native blooming plants that ensure continuous food sources across all seasons.

Why Choose Native Plants?

Native plants are the ecological basis upon which life depends. Here is how they revitalize our park.

Water Conservation

Once established, native plants require far less watering than lawns, helping us conserve local water resources.

Pollinator Habitat

Natives provide essential food and shelter for local butterflies, bees, and birds that non-native plants cannot offer.

Low Maintenance

Adapted to our local climate and soil, these plants are hardy and require less fertilizer and pesticide use.
