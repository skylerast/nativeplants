Planting Native In Small Spaces

For those working with limited space, plenty of native plants offer powerful wildlife advantages despite their modest size. Here are a few tips for planting in small spaces!

1
Use Sustainable Containers
If containers are part of your small-space garden, choose more sustainable options whenever possible. This means avoiding plastic containers and instead using pots made from recycled, reclaimed, or eco-friendly materials to reduce environmental impact.
2
Removing Invasives
One advantage of small-space gardening is that it makes managing invasive or non-native plants much easier. On large properties, removing these species can feel overwhelming and may take years or even decades to fully address.

Additional Habitat Components

Bird Feeders

There are many types of bird feeders designed for different foods. Tube, hopper, and tray feeders offer seeds, nectar feeders attract hummingbirds and orioles, and suet cages support woodpeckers and other insect-eating birds. Mealworms can be used in special feeders for birds that do not eat seeds.

Nesting Boxes

Many bird species depend on natural cavities in trees or branches to build nests, lay eggs, and raise their young. Nesting boxes, also known as birdhouses, mimic these natural shelters and provide birds with a safe and accessible place to live and reproduce.

Bee houses

Many bee species rely on small natural cavities in wood, stems, or soil to lay their eggs and shelter their young. Bee houses replicate these natural nesting spaces, providing a safe and convenient habitat that supports pollinators and helps maintain healthy ecosystems.
